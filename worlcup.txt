<p>The <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup" title="FIFA World Cup">FIFA World Cup</a> is an international <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">association football</a> competition established in <a href="/wiki/1930_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1930 FIFA World Cup">1930</a>. It is contested by the <a href="/wiki/List_of_men%27s_national_association_football_teams" title="List of men's national association football teams">men's national teams</a> of the members of <a href="/wiki/FIFA" title="FIFA">Fédération Internationale de Football Association</a> (FIFA), the sport's global governing body. The tournament has taken place every four years, except in 1942 and 1946, when the competition was cancelled due to <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. The <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">most recent World Cup</a>, hosted by Brazil in 2014, was won by <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_football_team" title="Germany national football team">Germany</a>, who beat <a href="/wiki/Argentina_national_football_team" title="Argentina national football team">Argentina</a> 1–0 after extra time.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The World Cup final match is the last of the competition, and the result determines which country is declared world champions. If after 90 minutes of regular play the score is a <a href="/wiki/Tie_(draw)" title="Tie (draw)">draw</a>, an additional 30-minute period of play, called <a href="/wiki/Extra_time" title="Extra time" class="mw-redirect">extra time</a>, is added. If such a game is still tied after extra time it is decided by kicks from the <a href="/wiki/Penalty_shoot-out_(association_football)" title="Penalty shoot-out (association football)">penalty shoot-out</a>. The winning penalty shoot-out area team are then declared champions.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> The tournament has been decided by a one-off match on every occasion except <a href="/wiki/1950_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1950 FIFA World Cup">1950</a>, when the tournament winner was decided by a final round-robin group contested by four teams (Uruguay, Brazil, Sweden, and Spain). Uruguay's 2–1 victory over Brazil was the decisive match (and one of the last two matches of the tournament) which put them ahead on points and ensured that they finished top of the group as world champions. Therefore, this match is regarded by FIFA as the <i>de facto</i> final of the 1950 World Cup.<sup id="cite_ref-All_results_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-All_results-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In the 20 tournaments held, 77 nations have <a href="/wiki/National_team_appearances_in_the_FIFA_World_Cup" title="National team appearances in the FIFA World Cup">appeared at least once</a>. Of these, 12 have made it to the final match, and eight have won.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>n 1<span>]</span></a></sup> With five titles, <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_football_team" title="Brazil national football team">Brazil</a> is the most successful World Cup team and also the only nation to have participated in every World Cup finals tournament.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_football_team" title="Germany national football team">Germany</a> have four titles. The other former champions are <a href="/wiki/Uruguay_national_football_team" title="Uruguay national football team">Uruguay</a> and <a href="/wiki/Argentina_national_football_team" title="Argentina national football team">Argentina</a> with two titles each, and <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a>, <a href="/wiki/France_national_football_team" title="France national football team">France</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_football_team" title="Spain national football team">Spain</a> with one each. The current champions, Germany, took their fourth title in 2014, and it is the first title for the Reunified German team. The 2014 German team also became the first European team to win in South America.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> The team that wins the finals receive the <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup_Trophy" title="FIFA World Cup Trophy">FIFA World Cup Trophy</a>, and their name is engraved in the bottom side of the trophy.<sup id="cite_ref-globo_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-globo-8"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/1970_FIFA_World_Cup_Final" title="1970 FIFA World Cup Final">1970</a> and <a href="/wiki/1994_FIFA_World_Cup_Final" title="1994 FIFA World Cup Final">1994</a> along with the <a href="/wiki/1986_FIFA_World_Cup_Final" title="1986 FIFA World Cup Final">1986</a>, <a href="/wiki/1990_FIFA_World_Cup_Final" title="1990 FIFA World Cup Final">1990</a> and <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup_Final" title="2014 FIFA World Cup Final">2014</a> games are to date the only matches competed by the same teams (Brazil–Italy and Argentina–Germany respectively). As of 2014, the <a href="/wiki/1934_FIFA_World_Cup_Final" title="1934 FIFA World Cup Final">1934 final</a><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>n 2<span>]</span></a></sup> remains the latest final to have been between two teams playing their first final. The final match of the upcoming <a href="/wiki/2018_World_Cup" title="2018 World Cup" class="mw-redirect">2018 World Cup</a> in Russia is scheduled to take place at the country's biggest sports complex, the <a href="/wiki/Luzhniki_Stadium" title="Luzhniki Stadium">Luzhniki Stadium</a> in Moscow.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>As of 2014, only nations from Europe and South America have competed in a World Cup Final.</p>
